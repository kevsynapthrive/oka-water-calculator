Water Pricing Calculator Help

The Oka' Institute Water Pricing Calculator helps rural and tribal water utilities plan sustainable water rates that:

  • Achieve full-cost recovery for operations, maintenance, debt, and infrastructure
  • Ensure affordability based on EPA guidelines (2.5% of Median Household Income)
  • Project long-term financial health for your water system

All calculations run in your browser - no data is stored on any server.

To get started quickly:

  1. Select a sample scenario from the dropdown that most closely matches your community
  2. Adjust the input values to match your specific situation
  3. Scroll down to see the automatically generated results

Tip: Use the Export button to save your work at any time. You can later use the Import button to reload all your data.

Full-Cost Recovery
Setting rates that cover all costs to operate, maintain, and eventually replace your water system.
MHI (Median Household Income)
The middle value of household incomes in your community - 50% of households earn more, 50% earn less.
EPA Affordability Threshold
The EPA suggests water bills should not exceed 2.5% of a community's MHI to remain affordable.
Tiered Rate Structure
A billing system that charges different rates for different levels of water usage.
O&M Costs
Operations and Maintenance costs - day-to-day expenses like staff, chemicals, electricity, and repairs.

  • Community Information: Population and income data needed for affordability analysis
  • System Information: Current customer count, usage, and water loss
  • Financial Information: Current costs, debt, and infrastructure needs
  • Loans and Debt: Add existing or planned loans
  • Capital Projects: Future infrastructure investments
  • Grants: Financial assistance that won't need to be repaid
  • Current Rate Structure: Your existing water rates
  • Future Rate Structure: Proposed changes to your rates

  • Bill Breakdowns: Shows exactly how bills are calculated under each rate structure
  • Affordability Analysis: Compares water bills to income levels
  • Revenue Status: Shows if your rates will generate enough money to cover expenses
  • Long-term Projections: Charts how costs and reserves will change over time
  • Water Loss Impact: Quantifies revenue lost due to leaks
  • Poverty-Level Affordability: Shows impacts on lower-income residents

Note: Red indicators show areas of concern that may need adjustment.
